Sustainability and smart cities



With the advancement of technology, we can see that the innovations it brings to us over the years can surprise us with how it is able to make our lives easier in a quick and smart way. And one of these innovations, being also one of the most highlighted in recent years are the Smart Cities.


Smart Cities aim to support human and social development, improving the quality of life of its citizens, being an urban space where their functionality revolves around Information and Communication Technologies (ICTs), using systems, devices and sensors that are responsible for the intelligence of these cities.

The main characteristics of Smart Cities are: human investment; social investments and modern ICT infrastructure; these are the drivers of sustainable economic growth that increases the quality of life and conscious management of natural resources.

Sustainability and technology work together when it comes to Smart City, this is because one of the main premises of Smart Cities is the minimization of environmental impacts. Smart cities refer to the concept of sustainability, they aim to promote a community environment, where everyone shares experiences and services.

Examples include Smart Cities: London, New York, Amsterdam, Paris and others. Brazil also has some Smart Cities, these are: Curitiba, Salvador and São Gonçalo do Amarante.
